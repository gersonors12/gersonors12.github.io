Reading #1- Kate Horsfield: Busting The Tube: A Brief History of Video Art

The push for an alternative way of thinking was a very important part of what the 1960s were all about. A challenging way of thinking was what a number of people were eagerly searching for and they began to question all aspects of society and why we had the ideals we had and why we thought the way we did. They wanted people to have more options than those offered at the time and they started experimenting with different mediums to bring these new ideas about. One of those new mediums was video. During the time, not everyone had access to video and the equipment and difficult editing system available at the time, but the artists and culture movers that were able to, started using the medium to experiment with new concepts to society that many, at first, found odd and unorthodox. However the methods were, video helped to challenge mainstream media by articulating ideas coming out of contemporary cultural art.
